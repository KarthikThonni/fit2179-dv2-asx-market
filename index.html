Behind the ASX 200

How Australia’s share market performed against the world over the last year, and which sectors and companies shaped its growth.

Data note: All market data was collected on 19 May 2026 at 6:15 PM. One-year returns therefore reflect the year leading up to that collection time.

1. Australia global market comparison

This world map compares one-year returns using country-based MSCI index tracking ETFs. Green countries recorded stronger returns, red countries recorded weaker returns, and blue-grey countries are not included in the dataset. The colour scale is capped at 80% to keep the comparison readable.

2. How did the ASX 200 move day by day?

This area chart shows the daily S&P/ASX 200 index level over the past year. The filled area highlights the overall movement of the index, making it easier to see periods of growth, decline and recovery across the year.

2. How has the ASX 200 performed over the past years?

This chart shows the yearly capital returns of the ASX 200 from 2015 to 2025.

3. Where are ASX 200 companies based?

This bubble map shows where ASX 200 companies are headquartered. Larger circles represent states with more companies, while a separate bubble shows companies headquartered outside Australia.

4. Which companies drive the ASX 200?

This treemap shows all ASX 200 companies grouped by sector. Rectangle size represents market capitalisation, while colour shows one-year return. Green rectangles show growth, red rectangles show loss, and larger rectangles represent higher-value companies.

5. Which sectors make up the ASX 200?

This chart shows the sector makeup of the ASX 200

6. Which ASX sectors grew the most?

This chart shows the spread of one-year company returns within each ASX 200 sector. Five extreme values above 300% were removed from this view so the main sector patterns are easier to compare. Four of the removed values were from Materials and one was from Health Care.

7. How concentrated is the ASX 200?

This chart ranks ASX 200 companies by market capitalisation and shows how quickly the largest companies add up to a major share of the index’s total value. The chart shows the top 50 companies for readability.

8. Does company size relate to one-year growth?

This scatter plot compares each ASX 200 company’s market capitalisation with its one-year return. A logarithmic x-axis is used so both smaller and larger companies can be compared more clearly. Five extreme values above 300% were removed from this view for readability.

9. Which sectors contributed most to ASX 200 growth?

This lollipop chart estimates each sector’s contribution to ASX 200 growth by combining company market capitalisation with one-year return. Positive values show sectors that added value, while negative values show sectors that reduced overall market value.

10. How did ASX sectors move over time?

This line chart compares daily ASX sector index movements over the past year. Each sector starts from a similar base, making it easier to compare which sectors rose, fell or stayed relatively stable over time.
